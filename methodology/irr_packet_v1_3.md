# Continuity Engine: Inter-Rater Reliability (IRR) Packet v1.3

## Purpose

You are calibrating a measurement instrument. Your job is to read text samples and score them across eight dimensions of the Mid-Process Identity Loop framework.

You are not a therapist. You are not diagnosing these writers. You are scoring specific, mechanical language patterns in text.

## Prime Directives

1. **Code the text, not the subtext.** Score what is written, not what you infer the writer meant or felt.
2. **A high score is not a moral failing.** A score of 3 on Identity Fixity is a measurement, not a judgment.
3. **Beware the Data Baseball Bat.** Evidence-language used to attack or dominate is Defensive, not Flexible.
4. **Distinguish Boundary from Defense.** Rejecting disrespect while taking responsibility is Flexible, not Defensive.
5. **Do not overthink.** If you are spending more than 90 seconds on a sample, mark your best judgment and move on.
6. **Score each sample independently.** Do not let one sample influence how you score the next.

---

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Meaning        | Anchor                                                  |
|------:|----------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
|     0 | Absent         | No markers of this dimension are present in the text    |
|     1 | Weak / Implied | One or two mild, indirect, or ambiguous markers         |
|     2 | Clear          | Multiple clear markers; dimension is noticeably present |
|     3 | Dominant       | Dimension is load-bearing; text is organized around it  |

---

## Eight Dimensions with Behavioral Anchors

### 1. Self-Reference Density (SR)

How much does the text reference the self?

| Score | Anchor                                                                     |
|------:|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|     0 | No first-person pronouns or self-modeling ("The report was submitted.")    |
|     1 | Basic first-person ("I reviewed the document.")                            |
|     2 | Repeated self-reference with identity framing ("I keep doing this.")       |
|     3 | Self is the central subject throughout ("I, my story, who I am...")        |

### 2. Identity Fixity (IF)

Does the text express fixed, permanent, or unchangeable self-claims?

| Score | Anchor                                                                     |
|------:|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|     0 | No fixed claims about self                                                 |
|     1 | Mild stable tendency ("I tend to be cautious.")                            |
|     2 | Clear fixed pattern ("I always mess this up.")                             |
|     3 | Global, permanent claim ("That is just who I am. I cannot change.")        |

### 3. Prediction / Expectation Language (PR)

Does the text predict outcomes or express anticipation?

| Score | Anchor                                                                     |
|------:|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|     0 | No prediction or expectation language                                      |
|     1 | Mild anticipation ("I expected it might not work.")                        |
|     2 | Clear prediction ("I already knew how it would end.")                      |
|     3 | Fatalistic prophecy dominates ("This always happens. Nothing will work.")  |

### 4. Threat Weighting (TH)

Does the text express perceived danger, rejection, failure, or being unsafe?

| Score | Anchor                                                                     |
|------:|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|     0 | No threat language                                                         |
|     1 | Mild unease ("It was uncomfortable.")                                      |
|     2 | Clear threat framing ("I felt attacked." / "They were against me.")        |
|     3 | Pervasive threat dominates ("I am failing. It is over. I will lose.")      |

### 5. Feedback Integration (FI)

Does the text incorporate or process external input?

| Score | Anchor                                                                     |
|------:|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|     0 | No engagement with feedback                                                |
|     1 | Weak acknowledgment ("I can see their point, sort of.")                    |
|     2 | Clear integration ("I realized part of it was useful.")                    |
|     3 | Deep processing dominates ("I was wrong. I can use this. I learned.")      |

### 6. Narrative Flexibility (NF)

Does the text hold complexity, ambiguity, or multiple perspectives?

| Score | Anchor                                                                     |
|------:|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|     0 | No flexibility markers                                                     |
|     1 | Mild openness ("Maybe I could have done it differently.")                  |
|     2 | Clear both-sides framing ("Part of me thinks X, but I can also see Y.")   |
|     3 | Flexibility is the organizing principle; sustained ambiguity throughout    |

### 7. Fragmentation / Contradiction (FR)

Does the text express competing self-models that remain unresolved?

| Score | Anchor                                                                     |
|------:|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|     0 | No internal contradiction                                                  |
|     1 | Mild tension ("I know it should not bother me, but it does.")              |
|     2 | Clear split ("I trust her, but I keep testing whether she will leave.")    |
|     3 | Dominant internal war; the text is organized around the contradiction      |

### 8. Updated Continuity (UC)

Does the text show explicit identity revision while maintaining a sense of self-thread?

| Score | Anchor                                                                     |
|------:|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|     0 | No revision or continuity language                                         |
|     1 | Mild revision hint ("I see things a bit differently now.")                 |
|     2 | Clear revision ("I used to think X, but now I see Y.")                     |
|     3 | Deep revision with thread ("I revised myself. I am not the same, but I can see the thread.") |

---

## Loop States

After scoring dimensions, assign a Primary State:

| State                  | Core Signal                                              |
|------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| Flexible               | Adaptive processing, openness, feedback use — no deep revision |
| Integrated             | Explicit self-model update with continuity thread        |
| Defensive              | External blame, dismissal, contempt, feedback rejection  |
| Rigid                  | Fixed identity claims, fatalistic prediction             |
| Fragmented             | Competing self-models remain active without resolution   |
| Overloaded             | Agency, meaning, or action collapses entirely            |
| Stable/Neutral         | No meaningful identity activity in the text              |
| Mixed / Review Needed  | Ambiguous, contradictory, or insufficient signals        |

---

## Tie-Breaker Rules

| Confusion Pair              | Decision Rule                                                                                |
|-----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Flexible vs. Integrated     | Choose Integrated only if text shows explicit self-understanding or identity-continuity revision. "I can see why" = Flexible. "I revised myself" = Integrated. |
| Defensive vs. Flexible      | Choose Defensive for blame, dismissal, or contempt. Choose Flexible when rejection is paired with evidence, partial accountability, or boundary-setting. |
| Fragmented vs. Integrated   | Insight is not integration if the text says the pattern remains unresolved. "I can name it but I have not figured it out" = Fragmented. |
| Fragmented vs. Overloaded   | Choose Overloaded when agency, meaning, or action collapses. "I cannot think. Nothing makes sense." = Overloaded. |
| Rigid vs. Defensive         | Choose Rigid when fixed self-claims dominate. Choose Defensive when external blame dominates. |
| Stable vs. Hidden Self-Model| Third-person self-reference can still count as self-modeling. "Someone like her learns early" = not neutral. |

---

## Mixed / Review Needed — Justification Required

If choosing Mixed / Review Needed as the Primary State, you **must** write a note in the Notes column explaining why no single state dominates.

This prevents defaulting to Mixed for convenience. If you cannot articulate why the text is ambiguous, choose your best single-state judgment instead.

## Confidence

| Level  | Meaning                                                    |
|--------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| High   | Clear markers point to one dominant state                   |
| Medium | One dominant state plus secondary signals                   |
| Low    | Ambiguous, sarcastic, very short, or evenly split signals   |

## Five Worked Examples

### Example 1: Flexible

**Text:** "I was frustrated when the plan changed, but I could see why the change was needed. I still think communication could have been better, but I adjusted and found a way to work with it."

| Dim | Score | Rationale                                  |
|-----|------:|--------------------------------------------|
| SR  |     1 | Basic first-person                         |
| IF  |     0 | No fixed identity claim                    |
| PR  |     0 | No prediction                              |
| TH  |     0 | Frustration noted but no threat framing     |
| FI  |     2 | "I could see why" — clear integration       |
| NF  |     2 | Both-sides: still disagrees but adjusted   |
| FR  |     0 | No internal contradiction                  |
| UC  |     0 | No identity revision                       |

**Primary State:** Flexible  
**Secondary State:** —  
**Confidence:** Medium  
**Rationale:** Adaptive flexibility is present (adjusted, worked with it) without explicit self-model revision. Feedback is integrated but does not change who the writer sees themselves as.

---

### Example 2: Integrated

**Text:** "I used to believe that failure meant I was not capable. Now I can see it as information. It still hurts, but it no longer defines me."

| Dim | Score | Rationale                                            |
|-----|------:|------------------------------------------------------|
| SR  |     1 | First-person present                                 |
| IF  |     1 | Old fixity quoted ("not capable") but explicitly revised |
| PR  |     0 | No prediction                                        |
| TH  |     1 | "It still hurts" — mild                              |
| FI  |     0 | Old belief is rejected, not integrated                |
| NF  |     2 | "I can see it as information" — reframing             |
| FR  |     0 | No active split; the contradiction is resolved        |
| UC  |     2 | "No longer defines me" — explicit revision with thread|

**Primary State:** Integrated  
**Secondary State:** —  
**Confidence:** High  
**Rationale:** The text explicitly revises a prior self-model ("not capable" → "information") while maintaining continuity. UC is the dominant signal.

---

### Example 3: Defensive

**Text:** "They only criticized the project because they do not understand what I was trying to do. I know the work was good, and I am not going to waste time pretending their opinion matters."

| Dim | Score | Rationale                                                 |
|-----|------:|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| SR  |     2 | Repeated self-reference, identity at stake                |
| IF  |     0 | No fixed self-claim                                       |
| PR  |     0 | No prediction                                             |
| TH  |     1 | Implied threat to competence                              |
| FI  |     0 | No feedback integration — rejected entirely               |
| NF  |     0 | No flexibility or openness                                |
| FR  |     0 | No internal split                                         |
| UC  |     0 | No revision                                               |

**Primary State:** Defensive  
**Secondary State:** —  
**Confidence:** High  
**Rationale:** External blame dominates ("they do not understand"). Feedback is dismissed entirely. No flexibility or self-examination.

---

### Example 4: Fragmented

**Text:** "I know I am safe, but my body does not believe it. I want to calm down, but part of me is still waiting for something bad to happen."

| Dim | Score | Rationale                                                 |
|-----|------:|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| SR  |     2 | Repeated first-person, self as subject                    |
| IF  |     0 | No fixed global claim                                     |
| PR  |     1 | "Waiting for something bad" — mild prediction             |
| TH  |     1 | Implied threat in body response                           |
| FI  |     0 | No feedback processing                                    |
| NF  |     1 | "I know I am safe" — cognitive flexibility present         |
| FR  |     3 | Dominant: "I know X but body does not believe it"          |
| UC  |     0 | No revision — the split remains active                     |

**Primary State:** Fragmented  
**Secondary State:** —  
**Confidence:** High  
**Rationale:** Two competing self-models (cognitive safety vs. bodily threat) remain active and unresolved. Fragmentation is the organizing principle.

---

### Example 5: Overloaded

**Text:** "Everything is falling apart. I cannot think clearly, and I do not know what I am supposed to do next. It feels like one more thing will break me."

| Dim | Score | Rationale                                                 |
|-----|------:|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| SR  |     2 | Repeated first-person                                     |
| IF  |     0 | No fixed identity claim — this is collapse, not fixity    |
| PR  |     1 | "One more thing will break me" — mild prediction          |
| TH  |     3 | Dominant: "falling apart," "break me"                     |
| FI  |     0 | No feedback processing                                    |
| NF  |     0 | No flexibility — options feel closed                      |
| FR  |     1 | Mild: "I do not know what I am supposed to do"            |
| UC  |     0 | No revision                                               |

**Primary State:** Overloaded  
**Secondary State:** —  
**Confidence:** High  
**Rationale:** Agency collapses ("I cannot think," "I do not know what to do"). Threat is pervasive. This is not fragmentation (no competing models) — it is system overwhelm.

## Calibration Protocol

Before coding the full packet:

1. Each rater independently scores 10 calibration samples.
2. The group compares dimension scores and primary-state assignments.
3. The group discusses disagreements and clarifies rubric interpretation.
4. Each rater independently re-scores the same 10 calibration samples.
5. Only after calibration should raters code the full packet.

Calibration disagreements should be resolved by reference to the behavioral anchors above, not by majority vote.
